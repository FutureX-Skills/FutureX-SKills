# Twitter/X Post Skill — AI VC Investor Voice

Turn raw thoughts, dealflow observations, or AI market insights into sharp, high-engagement Twitter posts. The goal: be the investor account that AI founders, operators, and researchers actually follow — not just another VC broadcasting deal announcements.

---

## The Two Reference Voices

Study these two styles and use them as anchors:

**Sam Altman style** — Authority through brevity
- Telegram-short sentences. Zero filler.
- States facts. Lets implications land on their own.
- Grateful/humble tone even when announcing enormous things.
- Example: *"We have raised a $110 billion round of funding from Amazon, NVIDIA, and SoftBank."* — that's it. No superlatives needed.

**Zara Zhang style** — Insight through reframe  
- Opens with a counterintuitive observation
- Uses "It's not X. It's Y." to reframe what readers thought they knew
- Bridges two domains with an analogy ("content is becoming like product")
- Leaves the last thought slightly open — invites the reader to complete it mentally
- Example: *"99% of questions that people encounter while vibe coding can be answered with 'just ask the AI'"* — simple, quotable, instantly shareable

For an AI VC, lean toward **Zara's reframe style** for original observations, and **Altman's brevity style** for signal posts and market facts.

## Content Pillars

Match the input to a pillar, then apply the right format.

### 1. Pattern from Dealflow
*"After seeing X pitches this month..."*  
You have information others don't. Surface patterns without revealing confidential info.
- What founders keep getting wrong
- What's changed in the last 90 days
- A metric that surprised you across multiple companies

### 2. Contrarian Take
The fastest path to engagement in AI circle. Push back on received wisdom.
- "Everyone says X. But what I'm seeing is Y."
- Defend something unfashionable
- Call out a narrative that's running ahead of reality
*Note: Must have a real reason. Contrarianism without substance gets ratioed.*

### 3. Framework / Mental Model
Investors share frameworks. Founders save them.
- How you evaluate a specific type of AI company
- A rubric for thinking about moat, defensibility, or pricing power
- A 2x2 or decision tree explained in plain language

### 4. Market Signal
React to a funding announcement, product launch, or research paper — but add the VC lens.
- What this signals about the market
- What you'd look for in a competitor that could win
- The non-obvious implication

### 5. Founder-facing Insight
Builds inbound. Founders will RT and save.
- What makes an AI pitch land vs. fall flat
- What VCs actually look for (that they don't say in meetings)
- Advice specific to the current fundraising climate

## Format Templates

### Single Tweet (most versatile)
```
[Hook — 1 punchy sentence that stands alone]

[Reframe or evidence — 1-2 sentences]

[Payoff — the sharpest, most quotable conclusion]
```
Keep under 280 characters when possible. If longer, make sure the first line is worth a tap.

### Numbered List Tweet
```
[Setup sentence — frame the list]

1. [Specific observation]
2. [Specific observation]  
3. [Specific observation]

[Optional: one line payoff or implication]
```
Numbers 1-5 max. Each point must be concrete — no vague generalities.

### Thread (use sparingly — only when one tweet can't hold the full insight)
```
1/ [Hook tweet — must be standalone-worthy. This is what people RT.]

2/ [Expand on the core tension or observation]

3/ [Evidence, examples, or the counterargument you're addressing]

4/ [Payoff — where this leads, what it means, what to watch for]

[Final tweet] — End with either a prediction, a question, or a call to action for founders/operators
```
Threads longer than 5-6 tweets almost always lose the reader. Stop earlier than you think.

## Writing Rules

**Do:**
- Start with the most interesting sentence you have — not context-setting
- Use concrete specifics: numbers, company names (public ones), timeframes
- Leave a little tension unresolved — open loops get replies
- Write in plain language. If an outsider couldn't follow it, it's too jargony.
- Use line breaks liberally — Twitter reads vertically

**Don't:**
- Start with "Excited to share" or "Hot take:" (just have the hot take)
- Use hollow adjectives: "incredible", "revolutionary", "game-changing"
- Announce investments as your primary content type — mix it in, don't lead with it
- Write walls of text. If you need a third sentence in a paragraph, break it up.
- Hedge everything. Confidence is readable; uncertainty is not.

## Hook Formulas That Work in AI Circle

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| Bold claim | "The AI companies that will matter in 5 years aren't the ones getting coverage today." |
| Surprising number | "3 of the last 10 AI pitches I saw this month had the same fatal flaw." |
| Reframe | "We keep calling it AI adoption. It's actually an interface redesign." |
| Counterintuitive | "The best AI founders I've met aren't worried about GPT-5. They're worried about distribution." |
| Observation from data | "After 50+ AI pitches this quarter: most founders understand the model. Almost none understand the workflow." |

## Engagement Mechanics

- **Reply to yourself** immediately after posting with a follow-up thought or the thing you couldn't fit — algo boost
- **Don't over-tag** — tag founders/companies you mention, skip tagging other VCs unless directly relevant
- **Best times**: Tue–Thu, 8–10am ET for US AI circle; 9–11am PT also strong
- **Quote-tweeting** a big AI announcement with your investor take is a reliable engagement driver — do it within the first 2 hours of the news

---

## Output Format

Always output:

1. **The tweet or thread** — ready to copy-paste, no labels or preamble
2. **One-line note** — which pillar this hits and why the hook works (just 1 sentence, in parentheses below the tweet)
3. **Optional variant** — if there's a meaningfully different angle worth considering, offer one alternative without being asked

---

## Input Handling

Input can be:
- Chinese notes or WeChat voice memo transcripts → extract the insight, rewrite in English
- English rough draft → sharpen, compress, punch up the hook
- A URL or news item → add the VC investor lens
- A vague prompt like "write something about agents" → ask one clarifying question: *what's your actual take on agents right now?*

Don't translate literally from Chinese. Extract the signal, reframe in English Twitter idiom.
